Consumer fraud

In June of 2002, I hired a contractor to remodel my kitchen. I gave him a check for $4,000 to buy supplies, cabinets, etc., and he never returned to do the work. I contacted the appropriate agencies –– Better Business Bureau, the State's Attorney's Office, the City of Chicago Department of Consumer Services –– attended two hearings, and was represented in one by a neighborhood attorney, but to no avail.

Then, Edwin McCullough took over the matter, and within four weeks, I had my cashier's check for $4,000. Before he stepped in, I had waited about one-and one-half years. Mr. McCullough's prompt action, thoroughness and dedicated approach to my problem got the results.

For that I am very grateful.

Karen M. Sholl
Chicago, Illinois
